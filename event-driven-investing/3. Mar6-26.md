## The World View: Why This Is an Inflection Point

### The Three Simultaneous Regime Changes

We are witnessing three structural regime changes occurring simultaneously -- each would be historic on its own. Together, they constitute an inflection point comparable to 2008 or 1973:

**1. The Geopolitical Regime Change**
The assassination of Iran's Supreme Leader and the effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz has shattered the post-1979 order in the Middle East. For 47 years, the Islamic Republic was a constant -- a hostile but predictable actor. Now there is a power vacuum. The Interim Leadership Council is governing a country under bombardment with 90% of its military capability destroyed. Russia and China condemned the strikes but offered no material support. The Gulf states condemned Iran's retaliation more than the original attack. The old order is gone. What replaces it is unknowable.

**The transmission to markets:** Oil is the mechanism. $85 Brent today; $100+ if Hormuz stays closed for weeks. This re-ignites inflation precisely when the Fed thought it was winning (2.4% CPI in January). The stagflation trap -- rising prices, slowing growth, paralyzed central bank -- is now the base case, not a tail risk. The Feb 24 document mapped this chain. It is now active.

**2. The Financial Regime Change**
Kraken's Fed master account is not just a crypto milestone -- it's the first structural breach in the wall between traditional banking and digital finance. Bank of America's CEO warning about $6.6 trillion in deposit migration is not hyperbole; it's math. When stablecoins offer 4%+ and banks offer 0.01%, the only thing preventing mass migration is regulation. And Trump just sided with crypto.

The stablecoin market at $317.94B growing toward $1T by year-end, combined with BTC ETF institutional flows of $458M in a single day during a literal war, signals that digital assets are being integrated into the financial system at the infrastructure level -- not just as speculative instruments. This is not 2017. This is not 2021. This is institutional rails being built.

**3. The Labor/AI Regime Change**
Block laying off 40% of its workforce and its CEO saying "most companies will do the same within a year" -- while the stock surges 24% -- is the single most disturbing data point in this entire report. The market is rewarding labor destruction. Amazon cut 16,000. Oracle is evaluating 20,000-30,000. 52,150 tech workers laid off in 2026 at 815/day. Meanwhile: 80% of firms report zero AI productivity gains, 56% of CEOs say they're getting "nothing," and hyperscalers are committing $660-690B in capex.

This is not creative destruction. This is destruction of labor AND destruction of capital, simultaneously, with no measurable output gains. The paradox from Feb 24 has deepened. The market is pricing in a future where AI replaces workers, but that future is not showing up in GDP, productivity, or earnings. The gap between narrative and reality has never been wider.
