## WHAT THE MARKET IS NOT PRICING: FIVE MISPRICINGS

### Mispricing #1: The Fertilizer-to-Food Fuse

~25% of globally traded nitrogen fertilizer and ~45% of sulphur exports transit Hormuz. Qatar's Ras Laffan (14% of global urea capacity) is shut. Urea up 27-44%. **Food futures have barely moved.** Wheat at $5.73. Corn up 5%.

This is the widest disconnect in the market. Fed/IMF research: food CPI lags oil by **8 quarters.** Four intermediate transmission links, each adding months. No sell-side CPI forecast captures this because they model next quarter. The impact arrives Q3-Q4 2026 into 2027.

**The irreversible trigger:** Planting decisions lock in by late April. If nitrogen is unavailable or above $800/ton, farmers plant soybeans instead of corn (soybeans fix their own nitrogen). This cannot be reversed once seeds are in the ground. The USDA Prospective Plantings report (March 31) is the first confirmation. After that, the trade gets crowded.

**Exploit:** Long corn / short soybeans spread (CBOT). Long ex-Gulf fertilizer producers (CF Industries, Nutrien, OCI, Yara) -- same product, 2x price, Gulf competitors offline. Q2 earnings will be explosive. **Window: now through March 31.**

### Mispricing #2: Oil-Import EM Equities

India imports 85% of its crude. Turkey 93%. South Africa 100%. All priced as growth stories -- India's Nifty at premium multiples, Turkey's BIST on a reform rebound. **The market has not connected $110+ oil to the macro destruction in oil-importing EMs.**

India's import bill rises ~$25-30B annually per $20/bbl increase -- 0.7% of GDP hitting the current account directly. RBI forced to tighten to defend the rupee, killing the growth story. Turkey worse: fragile lira, unconventional policy, zero buffer. In every oil shock since 1973, oil-importing EM currencies weakened 15-25% while DXY strengthened.

The INR has barely moved so far. The repricing comes when Q1 trade data publishes in April and RBI signals tightening -- by then it's front-loaded and violent.

**Exploit:** Short Indian equities (INDA puts), Turkish equities (TUR puts). Or via FX: short INR, short TRY against USD. 2-3 month trade with 20-40% downside if oil sustains above $100. **The timing advantage: this hasn't started yet.**

### Mispricing #3: Breakeven Inflation vs. Realized CPI

TIPS breakevens are at 2.39%. The market is pricing "transitory" oil pass-through that mean-reverts.

The oil shock will push headline CPI 1-2pp higher over the next 6 months (Phase 3 above). The fertilizer-to-food chain (Mispricing #1) extends the inflation impulse into 2027. Core PCE was already sticky at 3%+ before the war. Tariffs are additive. **Breakevens at 2.39% are pricing a world where none of this happens.** The gap between breakevens and incoming realized CPI is the widest it's been since early 2022.

This is a duration play. If the market is right that oil reverses in 3 weeks and everything normalizes, breakevens are fair. If we're right about 6-8 weeks and second-order cascade, breakevens need to be 3.5-4.0%. That's a massive move in TIPS.

**Exploit:** Long TIPS (TIP, VTIP). Short long-duration nominal Treasuries (TLT puts) -- stagflation destroys long bonds (1970s: -30%+ real). The spread between TIPS and nominal Treasuries widens as realized inflation prints above breakevens starting with the April 10 CPI report.
