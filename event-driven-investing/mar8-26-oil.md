## WHAT THE MARKET IS NOT PRICING: SIX MISPRICINGS

### Mispricing #1: The Gasoline Pass-Through Lag

**The gap:** Gas is at $3.45. Crude implies $4.00-$4.50 within 3 weeks based on the historical 50-60% pass-through over 20 working days. **The market sees $3.45 gas and thinks "manageable." It will be $4.50 before the month is over** -- and crude hasn't stopped rising.

**Why it matters:** Gas prices are the #1 driver of consumer inflation expectations (UMich). The March 13 UMich reading will spike. If 1-year expectations break above 4.5%, the Fed's credibility anchor cracks.

**How to exploit:** Gasoline crack spread futures benefit. More broadly, anything that benefits from higher-than-expected CPI: **TIPS, gold, short long-duration Treasuries (TLT puts).**

### Mispricing #2: The Fertilizer-to-Food Lag (Most Important)

**The gap:** ~25% of globally traded nitrogen fertilizer and ~45% of sulphur exports transit Hormuz. The world's largest single-site urea plant (Qatar's Ras Laffan, 14% of global capacity) is shut. Urea is already up 27-44%. **But food futures have barely moved** -- wheat hit an intraday high of $6.04 on March 2 but has settled back to $5.73. Corn only up 5%.

**Why it matters:** The fertilizer shock is arriving during Northern Hemisphere spring planting season. If nitrogen supplies don't reach the Corn Belt by April, farmers substitute soybeans for corn. **This decision is irreversible.** The crop mix is locked in at planting. The food price impact manifests in late 2026 through 2027 -- a **2-year lag** that no current CPI forecast captures.

**How to exploit:** **Long corn futures / short soybeans spread.** If the switch happens, corn tightens and soybeans loosen. Also: long fertilizer producers outside the Gulf (CF Industries, Nutrien, OCI, Yara) -- they sell the same product at much higher prices while their Gulf competitors are offline.

### Mispricing #3: The Duration Premium

**The gap:** Polymarket shows 61% ceasefire by March 31. Options markets are pricing a peak and reversal. **But the IRGC has 31 autonomous provincial commands specifically designed to sustain asymmetric warfare indefinitely.** Iran's mosaic defense doctrine was built for exactly this scenario. Trump's "unconditional surrender" demand has no precedent for ending a conflict with Iran. The US Navy can't escort tankers. Insurance markets won't normalize for weeks after any ceasefire.

**Why it matters:** Each additional week of Hormuz closure adds $5-10/bbl. A 6-week closure (through mid-April) means sustained $100-120 oil. An 8-week closure means $120-150. The market is pricing 3 weeks. If it's 6-8 weeks, every downstream effect doubles.

**How to exploit:** **Sell near-dated put spreads on oil (collect premium from those betting on reversal). Buy longer-dated calls.** The time decay of short-duration puts works in your favor if the crisis extends. Also: the contango in deferred oil contracts means the back of the curve hasn't priced sustained disruption -- buying June/July Brent calls is relatively cheap.

Software/tech (25-40% of private credit portfolios) faces BOTH the SaaSpocalypse and rising energy costs. If private credit funds draw simultaneously on bank revolving credit facilities, it creates systemic liquidity stress.

**How to exploit:** **Short BDCs (ARCC, MAIN, BXSL) and private credit managers (ARES, BX, APO) -- these are already -26% to -31% YTD but have further to fall if gating accelerates.** On the flip side: if private credit implodes, it could force a Fed liquidity response -- which would be wildly bullish for gold and risk assets simultaneously. ### Mispricing #6: The European Energy Crisis 2.0

## THE IEA SPR RELEASE: THE KNOWN COUNTER-CATALYST

**What's coming:** The IEA's informal threshold for coordinated SPR release is 2 weeks of Hormuz closure -- that's **March 16.** They may pre-announce as early as March 11-12. The release could be up to 180M barrels across member nations.

**Why it won't solve the problem:**
1. SPR releases are **temporary** -- they drain inventory without fixing the physical blockade
2. The US SPR is already at ~411M barrels (30% below 2022 pre-release levels). After the 2022 release (180M barrels), it took 3 years to partially refill. Political appetite for another massive drawdown is limited.
3. China's 1.4B barrels provide a buffer but Beijing is **hoarding, not releasing**
4. The math: 180M barrels / 9M bpd offline = **20 days of replacement.** It buys time, not a solution.

**The trading implication:** The SPR announcement will create a **1-3 day selloff in oil prices (Brent drops $5-10).** This is a gift. Buy the dip. The physical blockade persists, and the SPR release accelerates the depletion of the only strategic buffer left.
